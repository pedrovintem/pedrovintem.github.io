Exercise 2: Colouring Two Pitches

This exercise was challenging due to the limitation of using only two pitches. To maintain interest, I focused on the following aspects:

Rhythm: Varied rhythmic patterns and syncopation were crucial in creating momentum and interest.

Texture: The piece evolves from a sparse texture to a dense one, creating a sense of journey.

Timbre: The violoncello offers a warm foundation, while the marimba provides woody, percussive patterns. The flute contributes bright, agile movements in the upper register, contrasting with the glockenspiel’s sparkling, bell-like punctuations. The trombone adds a brassy tones.

Dynamics: The dynamic changes add expressivity.

This exercise reinforced the importance of elements beyond pitch in composition. It also highlighted how limitations can push my creativity, forcing me to explore other musical tools beyond pitches. In this case, the challenge of maintaining listener interest with just two pitches led to a greater focus on the rhymitcally interplay between instruments.
